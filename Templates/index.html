What is a Skin Lesion?

A skin lesion is any abnormal change in the skin's appearance, such as a growth, sore, rash, or discoloration. These can result from infections, injuries, or conditions like skin cancer. Common types include moles, warts, cysts, and rashes. While most are harmless, it's important to monitor for any changes that could indicate a serious condition.

Skin Lesion Types:

  • Actinic Keratoses (akiec): Precancerous skin lesion caused by sun damage.
  • Basal Cell Carcinoma (bcc): A type of skin cancer that originates in the basal cells.
  • Benign Keratosis (bkl): Non-cancerous lesions often associated with aging.
  • Dermatofibroma (df): Benign tumors of the skin, often small and firm.
  • Melanoma (mel): A serious type of skin cancer that develops in melanocytes.
  • Melanocytic Nevus (nv): Commonly known as moles, generally benign.

Symptoms of Skin Lesions

  • Change in size, color, or shape.
  • Itching, burning, or pain.
  • Bleeding or crusting of the lesion.
  • Sudden appearance of multiple lesions.

Prevention and Care

  • Sun Protection: Use sunscreen and wear protective clothing.
  • Healthy Habits: Maintain good hygiene and a balanced diet.
  • Routine Checkups: Monitor changes in skin lesions and consult a dermatologist if abnormalities occur.
  • Avoid Irritants: Reduce exposure to chemicals or allergens.
